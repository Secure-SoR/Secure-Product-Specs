  0. Executive Summary  

This specification defines the changes and additions required to extend Secure SoR to support Data Centres as a first-class asset type. It covers three distinct areas:

	•	Asset type template: property and space creation adapted for data centre topology (halls, suites, white floor, plant rooms, PODs)
	•	Dashboards: a new Data Centre Asset Manager dashboard set covering PUE, energy intensity, capacity, cooling, water, and ESG metrics
	•	SitDeck integration: connecting Secure SoR to the SitDeck DCIM platform (https://app.sitdeck.com) for operational telemetry, rack/device data, and real-time metering

This spec is structured for direct handoff to Cursor. Each section includes: what to build, how it differs from the existing office/retail model, exact schema changes, UI components, and integration contracts.

  1. Context & Strategic Fit  
1.1  Why Data Centres
Data centres are one of the most complex real estate asset types for sustainability and operational data management. They are:
	•	Energy-intensive: a single hyperscale facility can consume 50–200 MW
	•	Metering-rich: extensive submeter networks already exist but data is fragmented across DCIM, BMS, and FM systems
	•	Regulation-exposed: EU Energy Efficiency Directive, SEC climate disclosure, and emerging DC-specific reporting requirements
	•	Capital-markets relevant: DC valuations are increasingly tied to PUE, Renewable Energy Credits (RECs), and transition risk

Secure's System of Record architecture — asset-level data model, evidence chains, audit trail, AI agents — maps directly onto the data governance needs of DC asset managers and owners.

1.3  Relationship to Existing Architecture
The canonical Secure SoR layers remain unchanged. Data Centre is an additive asset type template that:
	•	Reuses: properties, spaces, systems, meters, data_library_records, evidence_attachments, agent_runs, audit_events
	•	Extends: asset_type enum, building_systems_taxonomy, space templates, dashboard module, agent context
	•	Adds: dc_metadata table (PUE targets, tier level, design capacity), dc_rack_assets table (SitDeck sync), new dashboard pages

	•	Forecast: capacity depletion date based on load growth trend (simple linear extrapolation)

	•	WUE trend: Water Usage Effectiveness by month — vs target

  6. SitDeck OSINT Integration  
6.1  What SitDeck Is
SitDeck (https://sitdeck.com) is an Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) dashboard platform. It aggregates 180+ live data providers across conflicts, geopolitical events, natural hazards, cyber threats, climate, weather, maritime, and markets onto a single customisable dashboard with 55+ drag-and-drop widgets and a 65-layer interactive world map. All data is sourced from free, publicly available feeds — government agencies, international organisations, and research institutions — with sub-minute refresh on critical feeds.
SitDeck is not a DCIM or energy monitoring tool. It is a situational intelligence layer. For Secure, it serves the Owner/Landlord account profile (the BMO use case) — an asset owner managing data centres as capital investments who needs to understand physical, geopolitical, and climate threats to those assets in real time, not just their operational performance.
6.2  Account Profile: Owner/Landlord (BMO Type)
The Owner/Landlord account type owns data centres as capital assets — typically an institutional investor, sovereign wealth fund, REIT, or infrastructure manager. Their primary concern is asset value preservation, risk-adjusted return, and regulatory disclosure. They are not running the facility day-to-day; they are making hold/sell/invest decisions and answering to lenders, insurers, and reporting frameworks (TCFD, SFDR, GRESB). SitDeck live intelligence is directly relevant to this profile for four reasons:
	•	Data centres are now military and geopolitical targets. AI infrastructure is critical to military operations; data centres in proximity to conflict zones, adversarial states, or critical national infrastructure corridors face materially different risk profiles than five years ago.
	•	Physical climate risk is immediate, not theoretical. Flood, wildfire, and extreme weather events near data centre assets are live events, not only long-term modelled scenarios. Owners need both real-time awareness and historical event evidence for insurance and TCFD disclosure.
	•	Lenders and insurers are asking. Physical and geopolitical risk is now a standard line item in DC acquisition due diligence, debt covenants, and ESG ratings. Secure’s audit trail means SitDeck-sourced risk events become documented evidence, not just situational awareness.
	•	The dashboards already proposed in Section 5 cover operational and ESG performance. SitDeck adds a real-world intelligence layer on top: the world outside the facility that affects the asset’s risk profile and value. The integration bridges operational data (Section 5) with external threat intelligence (Section 6).
6.3  New Dashboards: SitDeck Intelligence Layer
Three new dashboards are proposed for the Owner/Landlord profile, powered by live SitDeck feeds. These sit alongside the operational dashboards in Section 5 under a new “Risk Intelligence” tab in the Secure DC dashboard set. Routes:
	•	/dashboards/data-centre/:propertyId/geopolitical — Geopolitical & Conflict Risk
	•	/dashboards/data-centre/:propertyId/climate-hazard — Climate & Natural Hazard Risk
	•	/dashboards/data-centre/:propertyId/cyber-infrastructure — Cyber & Critical Infrastructure Risk
6.4  Dashboard A: Geopolitical & Conflict Risk
Audience: Asset owner and investment committee. Strategic question: is the asset in a geography whose risk profile has materially changed — and does that affect hold/sell/hedge decisions?
Context: Data centres are AI infrastructure. State and non-state actors have demonstrated intent and capability to target digital infrastructure as part of military and hybrid warfare campaigns. A hyperscale DC is both a strategic asset and a strategic target. The owner of such an asset needs live geopolitical intelligence mapped against their portfolio — not a quarterly risk report.

6.5  Dashboard B: Climate & Natural Hazard Risk

Context: Data centres are energy-intensive and physically fixed. Flood is the most acute threat — cooling systems, UPS, and generators are typically at ground level or in basements. Wildfire creates air quality issues affecting CRAC/CRAH intake filters and cooling efficiency. Extreme heat drives cooling load and PUE degradation. All of these are live, trackable events available via SitDeck from USGS, NOAA, and equivalent agencies.

6.6  Dashboard C: Cyber & Critical Infrastructure Risk
Audience: Asset owner, risk committee, IT/security function. Strategic question: is the digital and energy infrastructure the asset depends on under active threat — and has that threat been documented?
Context: Data centres are critical national infrastructure in most jurisdictions. CISA, NCSC, and equivalent agencies publish real-time advisories on threats to digital infrastructure. A DC owner needs to know when the sector their asset sits in is under active cyber campaign, grid attack, or supply chain compromise — even if their specific facility is not directly targeted. SitDeck aggregates CISA, NATO, and equivalent feeds directly.

6.7  Integration Architecture
SitDeck provides 180+ live data feeds via its platform. Secure does not replicate SitDeck data — it embeds SitDeck widgets (iframe or JS SDK) directly into the dashboard views, anchored to the property’s lat/lng coordinates. Structured risk events (threshold alerts that breached) are pulled via SitDeck custom alerts webhook and written to Secure’s agent_findings and audit_events tables.
Parameter
Detail
Widget rendering
Embedded iframe or JS SDK from SitDeck, rendered inside Secure dashboard panels. The user’s SitDeck session determines which widgets and layers are active — Secure passes the property lat/lng as the map centre point. Confirm embed method with SitDeck before build

SitDeck account token stored in Supabase secrets. One token per Secure account (not per property). All DC properties under the account share the same SitDeck connection

  7. AI Agents — Data Centre Extensions  
7.1  Existing Agents — DC Context
The two existing agents (Data Readiness, Boundary) must be extended to handle data centre context:

	•	Data Readiness Agent: add PUE, WUE, capacity, and cooling as new KPIs to assess. Check for SitDeck connection. Flag if no PUE data present.
	•	Boundary Agent: data centres have different boundary rules — IT load is always tenant; facility power includes shared services (cooling, lighting, security). The agent must understand the DC boundary model.
7.2  New Agent: PUE & Efficiency Advisor
A new agent specific to data centres. Triggered from the PUE dashboard or manually.

  10. SitDeck Physical Risk & Location Intelligence  
10.1  What This Is
This section covers a second, distinct SitDeck product that is separate from the SitDeck DCIM platform described in Section 6. SitDeck (https://sitdeck.com) is a physical risk intelligence and situational awareness platform that provides CIA-level geospatial dashboards for monitoring real-world events and hazards around physical assets. It operates as a widget-based map intelligence layer.
For Secure, this integration surfaces physical and climate risk directly on the data centre property view — showing the asset on a map alongside whichever risk overlay widgets the account holder has activated in their SitDeck account (flood zones, wildfire, extreme weather, geopolitical events, infrastructure outages, etc.). This data feeds directly into Secure’s existing Risk Diagnosis module as an external evidence source, with full provenance attribution.

10.7  How Risk Flows into Risk Diagnosis
The SitDeck physical risk map is not a decorative layer — the widget data feeds Secure’s audit-ready Risk Diagnosis module. The flow is as follows:
	•	Widget refresh: When the Secure backend refreshes sitdeck_risk_config.active_widget_types, it reads the list of active risk layer types from the SitDeck account.
	•	Risk flag write: Each active widget type is written to the property’s risk record as a physical_risk_flag entry with: risk_domain (flood | wildfire | extreme_weather | grid_outage | seismic | geopolitical), source = ‘sitdeck’, evidence_ref pointing to the sitdeck_risk_config row, and recorded_at timestamp.
	•	Audit trail: Every write to physical_risk_flags from SitDeck produces an audit_event entry (action: risk_flag_updated, source: sitdeck, property_id). This ensures the risk profile is traceable and defensible for TCFD, SFDR, and lender due diligence.
	•	No hardcoding: Secure never assumes which risk types are active. The risk profile of a property is always derived from what the user has actually configured in their SitDeck account — not from a fixed risk taxonomy. Adding a new widget in SitDeck automatically propagates to the property’s risk record in Secure on the next refresh.
